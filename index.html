Tribute to Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam

Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam was an eminent scientist, statesman, and the 11th President of India. He was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu. Dr. Kalam played a pivotal role in India's nuclear and space programs, earning him the nickname "Missile Man of India."

Apart from his scientific contributions, Dr. Kalam was known for his inspirational speeches and his efforts to ignite young minds. He believed in the power of education and urged the youth to dream big and work hard to achieve their goals.

Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam received numerous awards and honors, including the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian award. He passed away on July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management, Shillong.

Let us remember and honor the legacy of this extraordinary individual who dedicated his life to the betterment of our nation.
